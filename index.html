About the Novel

The Picture of Dorian Gray by Oscar Wilde explores the consequences of vanity, aestheticism, and moral corruption.

"The only way to get rid of temptation is to yield to it."

Main Themes

  • Beauty and Youth
  • The Corruption of the Soul
  • The Influence of Lord Henry
  • Art vs. Reality
  • Morality and Consequence

Interpretation & Analysis

Dorian's portrait represents the hidden consequences of unchecked desire. Wilde critiques Victorian moral hypocrisy while exploring aesthetic philosophy.
